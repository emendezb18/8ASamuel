NEWS ABOUT THE CONFLICT

1.Sept 8 1945 YANKS OCCUPY SOUTH KOREA Thousands of American troops began the occupation of Korea today. Pacific veterans of the Seventh division splashed ashore from landing craft at Inchon (Jin-sen) on the west coast of Korea preparatory to occupying the capital city of Keijo. Russian forces are occupying northern Korea. Japanese civil police fired Into a crowd of Korean demonstrators in front of the Inchon city hall shortly after the American landings.The Japanese who are policing the area until the occupation is completed, said the Koreans were "communist demonstrators." The American flag will be raised formally on the atoll Monday.

2.June 25 1950 NORTH KOREANS INVADE SOUTH KOREA Troops from Communist-dominated North Korea launched a series of attacks across the border into American-backed South Korea early Sunday, fragmentary reports from the frontier said. Reports from the 38th parallel, which divides the Northern and Southern parts of Korea, said that attacks were launched generally along the border. They said the North Koreans were supported by tanks in the Chunchon area, 50 miles northeast of Seoul. The border area has been the scene of frequent guerilla clashes between North and South Koreans.

POLITICAL CHANGES AFTER THE WAR

Political Changes After The War:

DIVISION OF KOREA: The Korean War led to the division of Korea into two separate states along the 38th parallel North Korea (Democratic People's Republic of Korea) and South Korea (Republic of Korea) The division deepened the ideological and political differences between the communist North, supported by the Soviet Union and China, and the anti-communist South, supported by the United States and its allies.

CHINESE INVOLVEMENT: China's entry into the war on the side of North Korea had significant consequences. The intervention of Chinese forces prolonged the conflict and increased the scale of the war. It also heightened tensions between the United States and China, contributing to a long period of hostility between the two countries during the Cold War.

STRENGTHENING OF THE COLD WAR: The Korean War was one of the early conflicts of the Cold War, pitting communist forces against anti-communist forces. The war heightened tensions between the United States and its allies on one side and the Soviet Union and China on the other. The Cold War rivalry became more pronounced in Asia, influencing subsequent events in the region.

MILITARY ALLIANCES: The Korean War had a lasting impact on military alliances in the region. The United States solidified its alliances with countries like South Korea and Japan, establishing a strong military presence in the Asia-Pacific region.

ARMISTICE AGREEMENT: The Korean War ended in 1953 with an armistice agreement, not a formal peace treaty. This meant that technically, North and South Korea remained in a state of war.

Political boundaries of all the participants before and after: The Korean War (1950-1953) involved several countries and had significant geopolitical consequences, particularly for the Korean Peninsula.

1.North Korea before: Before the war, North Korea was part of a united Korea under Japanese rule until the end of World War II in 1945. After the war, the Korean Peninsula was divided along the 38th parallel into two occupation zones, with the Soviet Union occupying the north and the United States occupying the south. after: The Korean War ended in 1953 with an armistice agreement, not a peace treaty. As a result, the Korean Peninsula remained divided along the 38th parallel. North Korea remained a separate, communist state under the leadership of Kim Il-sung.

2. South Korea before: Before the war, South Korea was also part of a united Korea under Japanese rule until 1945. After the war, the southern part of the Korean Peninsula came under U.S. influence. After: The southern part of the Korean Peninsula, under the influence of the United States, became the Republic of Korea (ROK), a separate, non-communist state

3.United States before: The United States played a major role in the southern part of the Korean Peninsula after World War II, occupying the territory below the 38th parallel. After: The U.S. maintained a military presence in South Korea and continued to provide security assistance to the South.

4.Soviet Union: The Soviet Union occupied the northern part of the Korean Peninsula after World War II, establishing a communist government in North Korea.

5.China Intervened in the Korean War in support of North Korea. The war solidified the border between China and North Korea.
