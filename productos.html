Timeline korean war

June 25, 1950: Outbreak of War

  • North Korean forces, under Kim Il-sung, invade South Korea, marking the beginning of the Korean War.

    • June 27, 1950: United Nations Intervention

  • The United Nations Security Council adopts a resolution calling for member states to assist South Korea, leading to the deployment of international forces.

    • September 15, 1950: Inchon Landing

  • General Douglas MacArthur leads a successful amphibious landing at Inchon, cutting off North Korean forces and changing the course of the war.

    • October 19, 1950: Chinese Intervention

  • The Chinese People's Volunteer Army enters the war, supporting North Korea and pushing UN forces back.

    • January 4, 1951: Stalemate and Ceasefire Talks

  • The front lines stabilize near the 38th parallel, and ceasefire talks begin at Panmunjom.

    • July 27, 1953: Armistice Agreement

  • An armistice agreement is signed, officially ending active fighting in the Korean War. The Korean Peninsula is divided along the 38th parallel.

    • 1954-1966: Demilitarized Zone (DMZ) Established

  • The DMZ is established as a buffer zone between North and South Korea, monitored by the United Nations Command and the Korean People's Army.

    • 1956: Korean Demilitarized Zone Incidents

  • Tensions flare with a series of incidents along the DMZ, heightening the risk of renewed conflict.

    • 1966-1969: Korean DMZ Conflict

  • Skirmishes and clashes occur along the DMZ, contributing to ongoing tensions between North and South Korea.

    • 1972: Panmunjom Agreement

  • North and South Korea sign the Panmunjom Declaration, aimed at reducing military tensions and promoting dialogue.

    • 1991: Inter-Korean Basic Agreement

  • North and South Korea sign an agreement to reconcile and promote peaceful reunification, including the denuclearization of the Korean Peninsula.
